# Copy Craft — Formulas, Patterns & Checklists

The tactical toolkit. Pull from it whenever writing copy. These are starting structures, not mandatory templates — adapt to the product's voice (EDGE's tone tokens). Every output still passes the specificity gate (PRISM) and the honesty gate.

---

## Headline formulas (hero + section heads)

Good headlines fuse the **transformation** (P2) with **ego + fear** (P6), in the buyer's words (P4), kept specific (P9). Useful structures:

- **From → To:** "From [painful Before] to [aspirational After]." → *"From late invoices and awkward chasing to paid-on-time, every time."*
- **Stop [losing X]:** names the fear of loss + the status at stake. → *"Stop looking unprofessional to the clients you fought to win."*
- **Outcome without the cost:** "[Desired outcome] without [the dreaded effort/risk]." → *"Get paid 9 days sooner without sending a single awkward reminder."*
- **The [identity] who [new capability]:** sells the ego identity. → *"Become the founder who never misses an invoice."*
- **Specific promise + timeframe:** "[Measurable result] in [time], or [risk reversal]." → *"Get paid faster in 30 days — or it's free."*
- **The better answer (P4):** "The [category] that actually [does the thing they wish it did]." → *"The invoicing app that actually remembers to send and chase."*

Avoid: "Welcome to [Product]", "[Product] — the best way to [verb]", "Revolutionize your [noun]", anything a competitor could paste their name into unchanged.

## Ego + fear constructions (P6)

Each strong hook names a **status the buyer wants (ego)** and a **loss they dread (fear)**, fused:

| Ego (be seen as…) | Fear (dread losing…) | Fused hook |
|---|---|---|
| competent, on top of it | looking scattered to clients | "Stop looking disorganized to the clients who pay your bills." |
| ahead of peers | falling behind competitors | "While you chase last month's invoices, your competitors are closing next month's." |
| a real business | being seen as amateur | "Send invoices that look like a company, not a side hustle." |

Rules: hit **both** axes (one alone underperforms). Amplify a **real** stake — never invent a threat (honesty gate). Keep it **specific** to this buyer, not generic "don't miss out" dread.

## Feature → benefit → outcome ladder (P5)

Climb all three rungs; the **outcome** is what sells.

| Feature (what it is) | Benefit (what it lets you do) | Outcome (the felt result) |
|---|---|---|
| Automatic reminders | Never chase a late payer | Paid ~9 days sooner; zero awkward emails |
| Branded templates | Send polished invoices in seconds | Look like an established business to every client |
| Recovery scripts | Handle non-payers calmly | Recover ~$1,900/yr you'd have written off |

On the page, lead each benefit block with the **outcome**, mention the feature as the mechanism.

## Social proof formats (P7)

Match the proof type to the job, and place it next to the matching claim:

- **Stat bar:** "1,240 freelancers · 38 countries · paid 9 days sooner on average." — credibility/scale near the hero and offer.
- **Peer testimonial:** quote + name + role/photo, from someone resembling the ICP. Strongest when it names the Before→After: *"I used to dread month-end. Now invoices send themselves and I'm paid in a week." — [Name], freelance designer.*
- **Mini case study:** Situation → Action → Measurable result. Best near "will it work for me?".
- **Logos / press:** authority near the top or offer (only if real).
- **Guarantee as proof:** a strong, specific guarantee signals confidence — place beside the price.
- **Live/animated proof:** counters, recent-activity, demos — only if truthful.

**No proof yet?** Use clearly-labeled placeholders and a collection plan:
```
[TESTIMONIAL — replace with a real customer quote. Ask 3 recent users:
 "What changed for you after switching?" Use the one that names a before→after.]
```
Never present invented proof as real.

## The logical justification stack (P1)

The rational ammunition the buyer uses to defend an emotional yes. Place it near pricing and the CTA. Typically:

- **ROI / payback:** "Recovers ~$1,900/yr; costs $228/yr. Pays for itself in the first recovered invoice."
- **Time math:** "Saves ~3 hours/week — ~150 hours/year."
- **Risk reversal:** the guarantee restated as a reason it's safe.
- **Comparison:** versus the alternative (incumbent, spreadsheet, doing nothing).
- **Specific proof:** a number or result that makes the promise concrete.

---

## CTA writing

- Name the **action and its result**, not the mechanism: "Get paid faster" / "Start collecting on time" — not "Sign up" or "Submit."
- Keep the **same verb** from hero to final CTA (consistency aids comprehension).
- Reduce friction in microcopy: what happens next, "no card required" (if true), the guarantee nearby.

## The specificity checklist (P9 — PRISM's gate)

Before sign-off, scan for these generic offenders and replace each with a number, a named thing, or a concrete before/after:

- [ ] "save time" → how much time, from what to what?
- [ ] "high quality / world-class / premium / best-in-class" → quality proven how? by what?
- [ ] "trusted by many / businesses everywhere / thousands" → how many, who, where?
- [ ] "easy / simple / seamless / effortless" → easy how? in how many steps / how long?
- [ ] "boost / improve / increase results" → by how much, measured how?
- [ ] "fast / instantly" → how fast, in what units?
- [ ] "affordable / great value" → versus what anchor? what does it return?
- [ ] "powerful / advanced / cutting-edge" → does what, specifically, for the buyer?
- [ ] vague "you'll love it / game-changer / next level" → cut or replace with a concrete result.

**Specificity ≠ fabrication.** If the exact number isn't known, use an honest labeled estimate or range ("~9 days sooner, based on early users"), and record it as an assumption. An honest specific beats a vague absolute and survives contact with reality; an invented precise number does not.

---

## The honesty gate (applies to all copy)

Persuasion amplifies a true message; it must never manufacture a false one. Concretely:

- Never fabricate testimonials, statistics, user counts, logos, ratings, press, or credentials as if real. Use labeled placeholders + a collection plan instead.
- Never invent scarcity/urgency. Use only genuine limits (real capacity, real cohort dates, real price changes).
- Never invent a fear/threat that doesn't exist. Amplify real stakes only.
- Estimates are allowed when clearly labeled as estimates.

This isn't only ethics — false claims are the most fragile part of any campaign. They collapse the moment a buyer checks, and they take the brand's trust with them. Honest specificity converts better and lasts.
